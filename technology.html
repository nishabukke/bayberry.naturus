Supercritical fluid extraction (SFE) is a separation process that utilizes supercritical fluids as the solvent to extract target compounds from various substances. A supercritical fluid refers to a substance that is maintained at a temperature and pressure above its critical point, where it exhibits both liquid and gas-like properties. In the case of SFE, carbon dioxide (CO2) is commonly used as the supercritical fluid due to its favorable properties.

Apart from being solvent residue free, the supercritical fluid extraction is a green process that is highly rated for its eco-friendliness. Supercritical extraction using CO2 is considered organic compatible and is widely used in the manufacturing of organic certified products.

Advantages

:Selectivity:

Tunable selectivity for precise extraction of target compounds.

:Mild operating conditions:

Low temperatures to preserve heat-sensitive compounds.

:Closest to the natural product:

Products obtained is closest to the aroma and other properties of the original botanical material.
No Hydrolysis or destruction of temperature sensitive active ingredients

:Environmentally friendly:

Non-toxic and non-flammable CO2 as a solvent.
No solvent residue left behind.

:Efficiency and productivity:

Rapid process with high extraction efficiencies.

:Versatility:

Wide range of substances can be extracted.
Extraction of compounds with varying properties and volatilities

:Residue-free extracts:

Complete removal of the supercritical fluid.
Produces residue-free extracts.
Generally regarded as safe product(GRAS)
